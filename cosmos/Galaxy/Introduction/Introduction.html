Introduction


A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter.
The word galaxy is derived from the Greek galaxias, literally "milky", a reference to the Milky Way.
Galaxies range in size from dwarfs with just a few billion (109) stars to giants with one hundred trillion (1014) stars,
each orbiting its galaxy's center of mass.
